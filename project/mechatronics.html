Development of Active Front Wheel Steering System for Passenger Vehicle due to Side Wind Force Disturbance

Lateral wind forces displace the vehicle out of its intended trajectory to the opposite lane, leading to road accident if there is an oncoming vehicle. This research aims to develop an Active Front Wheel Steering (AFWS) system to stabilize a 9-DOF passenger vehicle laterally by correcting the steering angle via the yaw rate (the rate of vehicle turning sideways). All modelling and simulations were conducted on MATLAB/SIMULINK. [IJAME journal] open_in_new

Vehicle dynamics
MATLAB/SIMULINK
  • Modelled the passenger vehicle (2-DOF rack and pinion steering model, Pacejka tire model, load distribution model, handling model, lateral and longitudinal slip model) with vehicle dynamics equations.

  • Verified the passenger vehicle model with data from CarSim and IPG CarMaker.

  • Developed and tuned three control systems: PID, Fuzzy-tuned PID, and Fuzzy logic controllers. Compared the controllers' performance in stabilizing the vehicle under disturbance.

  • Developed a yaw rate observer to estimate the yaw rate of non-linear vehicle model for non-zero steering inputs using empirical data. This enables disturbance testing on non-zero steering conditions.

Solving a 3-disc Tower of Hanoi with a 4-DOF Robotic Arm - Graphical Simulation

A 4-DOF uArm-inspired robotic arm solving a Tower of Hanoi puzzle. The robot is modelled with Peter Corke's Robotic Toolbox. The forward kinematics are designed with D-H parameters. The inverse kinematics are designed with trigonometry. All visuals are simulated with MATLAB.

A Fully Automated PLC Sorting Station with Freight Unit

A sorting station which rejects unsuccessful peg transfers and load the successful ones onto an autonomous freight unit via a robotic arm. This project showcases the beauty of factory automation.

  • Designed a 4-DOF robotic arm with SolidWorks CAD and fabricate it with a mix of machining and 3D printing. The robot moves with servo motors.

  • Designed the logic of the sorting station, the locomotion of the robotic arm, and the freight robot using Arduino programming.

  • Developed a line following robot based on infrared sensors as the freight unit.

A Pneumatic Pick & Place Station

Pick and place a sponge cube onto a cargo robot using pneumatic linear actuators. The station features three major sectors: a gate to control the moving/stopping of the cargo robot, the main pick and place unit, and a unit to separate the sponge cube from the pick and place unit.

Teamwork
  • Designed the pneumatic linear actuator with SolidWorks CAD software.

  • Simulated stress test on the internal piston of the actuator with SolidWorks.

  • Prepared detail drawings and bill of materials. All parts are fabricated with machining (lathing, milling, drilling, bending etc.).

  • Designed other auxiliary parts, such as the gate, platform attachment, the sliding overhead passage, and the cube separator.
