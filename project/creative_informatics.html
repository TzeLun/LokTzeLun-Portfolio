A Surgical Bone Drilling Virtual Reality Simulator with Realistic Real-time Drilling Audio Generator

A bone drilling simulator designed to supersede the use of live specimens in animal experiments as a source for synthetic training data generation and a test bed for robotic control algorithms.

Summary of research work:

Demonstration and setup:

  • Developed the VR simulator in C++ through CHAI3D and AMBF. Graphics are rendered using texture-based volume ray-casting with OpenGL and shader programming. Drilling models are prepared as volumetric data from CT DICOM data and stl files via 3D Slicer.

  • Designed a communication protocol between the VR simulator (C++) and the audio generator (Python) using subscriber-publisher from Robot Operating System (ROS). The simulator sends drill force, surface dryness, and drill angle data to the generator as generation conditions.

  • Control the virtual drill using Phantom Omni or Geomagic Touch X haptic device. Haptic feedback such as drill weight, drill force, and drill vibration are perceivable. Drill force is estimated with the finger-proxy algorithm whereas the vibration is modelled as a gaussian noise.

  • Developed the audio generator based on deep learning using PyTorch library. (More details under the deep learning section). The audio generator could generate realistic audio in real time in response to the drilling scenario.
